Bio

I’m currently an MASc candidate in Systems Design Engineering at UWaterloo’s Vision and Image Processing Lab, advised by Prof. Alexander Wong and Prof. Javad Shafiee. My research has focused on projects at the intersection of efficient ML (specifically applying neural architecture search to NeRFs and vision transformers), embedded systems (latency prediction for on-device inference), and robotics (building simulation pipelines for synthetic data generation and training embodied morphologies).

I previously graduated from the University of Waterloo with a BASc in Mechatronics Engineering with an Option in Artificial Intelligence. As an undergraduate, I’ve interned as a software engineer across the full systems stack including sensor processing and communications infrastructure on self-driving cars, simulators for robot grasping, real-time video analytics applications for embedded microcontrollers, safety-critical bootloader firmware for aerospace products, silicon validation for AI accelerators, and web development for e-commerce.

I’m interested in developing robots that can learn to interact with the world in an open-ended manner. I’m a big proponent of using simulations for training embodied agents, and in the near-term I see physics-based virtual environments as rich data sources, ideal for bootstrapping agents with the ability to handle complex embodied tasks. However, to reach a level of learning and power efficiency comparable to biological systems, I believe we must eventually reimagine our current approaches to agent learning and adopt a paradigm in which the neural architecture itself is generated. I aim to draw inspiration from pioneering systems exhibiting early signs of open-ended learning in simulations — studying both the emergent behaviours and the methods enabling them. By harnessing these insights, I seek to develop frameworks that can leverage open-ended creativity and apply it to not just find efficient architectures in fixed search spaces, but to generate novel, previously unexplored classes of architectures.

I take pride in being a strong generalist and enjoy working across roles and disciplines. I’m looking for new opportunities to build the next generation (and beyond) of robotics. If you’re working on something interesting, or want to learn more, I’d love to chat!
